Why does Jem want to go back to get his pants? What does his reason tell you about his relationship with his father? Cite a specific quote to support your answer.

The reason that Jem is determined to retrieve his pants from the Radley’s yard was because he did not want to disappoint his father. Atticus upholds strong beliefs and morals and due to Jem’s age, he is expected to follow and adhere to them - Jem did not want to hurt Atticus’s feelings. The quote “I don’t want to hear of poker in any form again. Go by Dill’s and get your pants, Jem. Settle it yourselves” backs this up as it highlights the strict relationship that Jem has with his father.
